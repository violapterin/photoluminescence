🙝 homage to Mika Nakashima

I used to think that I had better die,

Because I failed to tie a lace of shoe,

The more I think of it, the harder try.

When by the piers the seagulls called in sky,

Which lingered over ocean waves of blue,

I used to think that I had better die.

I try to get to somewhere faraway,

To take the train amidst the mountain view,

The more I think so, when I harder try.

Beneath the plum tree blossoming I lie,

Perhaps I am a rotten petal too,

The time I think that I had better die.

If only we had passed each other by,

If there were some good hearted ones like you,

The more I think of it, the harder try.

Your radiant smile had made me want to cry,

To live another day in hopes; it’s true

I try to stop the thought that I should die,

But more I think so, when I harder try.
